About me

Hi, I'm Slawomir.

And piping engineer.
Who?...

Well, piping is just about pipes. Small and big ones. Diameter 15mm up to 1000mm and above. Easy topic? Yes, it's only about straight pipes?

Lets take power plant for example. How long are pipes inside one powerplant? 1 km? 10km? Could be even more than 100 km.

Steel pipes, stainless steel pipes, plastic pipes. Which wall thickness? With or without insulation? Heat tracing? Personal protection? Should it be lined inside? Rubber, glass? What flows inside? Water, steam, natural gas, air, compressed air, process air, slurry. What is the fluid pressure and temperature value? What pressure rating flanges we need? Would pipe supports carry the load? Do we need anchor points? Do pipes have enough flexibility? Is there enough space between pipes and electric trays? Is steel structure strong enough? Seems overwhelming. Yes, it is. Even after dozen years of experience in this field there is still a lot to learn. Industry is still moving on, new technologies are continuously shaping this proffesion. All of above and much more need to be done to complete a plant piping. This is a job for well organized team. Tasks are divided between specialized engineers. I'm one of them.

Beside brain my tools to achive goals is software such as Plant 3D and CADWorx Plant Design. Both are based on databases. One out of many task is to get reports like quantity material report, clash report, isometric drawing list. Another is design simple (doesn't mean easy) 3D piping plant layout. Both greenfield and brownfield projects. First is built from scratches, second one is rebuilding existing installations.

Last but not least, I'm recording short screencasts about efficient use of AutoCAD to share knowledge and best practices.

There are many similarities between frontend developer job and piping engineer. Because of that I learned HTML, CSS and JS.
