About me

Hi, I'm Slawomir. I'm UX designer and piping engineer.

Why I'm explaining about piping engineering? Because in its roots it's very similar to UX designer job. You will see. It's just about pipes. Small and big ones. Diameter 15mm up to 1000mm and above. Easy topic? Yes, it's only about straight pipes?

Lets take power plant for example. How long are pipes inside one powerplant? 1 km? 10km? Could be even more than 100 km.

Steel pipes, stainless steel pipes, plastic pipes. Which wall thickness? With or without insulation? Heat tracing? Personal protection? Should it be lined inside? Rubber, glass? What flows inside? Water, steam, natural gas, air, compressed air, process air, slurry. What is the fluid pressure and temperature value? What pressure rating flanges we need? Would pipe supports carry the load? Do we need anchor points? Do pipes have enough flexibility? Is there enough space between pipes and electric trays? Is steel structure strong enough? Seems overwhelming. Yes, it is. Even after dozen years of experience in this field there is still a lot to learn. Industry is still moving on, new technologies are continuously shaping this proffesion. All of above and much more need to be done to complete a plant piping. This is a job for well organized team. Tasks are divided between specialized engineers. I'm one of them.

Beside brain, my tools to achive goals is software such as Plant 3D and CADWorx Plant Design. Both are based on databases. One out of many task is to get reports like quantity material report, clash report, isometric drawing list. Another is design simple (doesn't mean easy) 3D piping plant layout. Valves, operators, pumps, tanks pressure indicators and other equipment need to be placed where man can reach it easily and operate smoothly. In general whole plant need to be user friendly nad safe.

User experience is a essential part of design process. Each designer is responsible for it. Placing everything on drawing is next milestone. It's a lot work to give clean, meaningful and easy to understand layouts, isometric drawings, arrangements.

Thank you for reading.
